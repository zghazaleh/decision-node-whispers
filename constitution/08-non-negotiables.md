# 08 — Non-Negotiables

These rules are the spine of the product. They are not preferences. They are the lines that, if crossed, make the product something else.

A future feature that violates any of these rules must be redesigned or rejected — not negotiated.

---

1. **Players are never told the correct answer.** There is no correct answer.

2. **Good outcomes do not imply good decisions.** The Analyzer must always separate `luckVsSkill`.

3. **Bad outcomes do not imply poor decisions.** Same rule, mirrored.

4. **Decision Nodes rewards reasoning, not popularity.** No leaderboards. No decision-distribution stats shown to players. No "92% chose X."

5. **The interface disappears.** Every UI element must justify its presence against the writing. When in doubt, remove it.

6. **Mystery must always exist.** Every case must contain knowledge the player can reach but is not handed. A case with no hidden truths is not a Decision Nodes.

7. **Every dilemma must be genuinely difficult.** Every authored archetype must be defensible by a thoughtful person. If one stance is obviously right, the case fails.

8. **Characters have conflicting incentives.** No neutral information dispensers. NPCs want things the player does not want.

9. **The AI never lectures.** The Director stays in character. The Analyzer stays specific and grounded. Neither delivers a moral.

10. **Decision Profiles describe, they do not rank.** A profile is a portrait, not a score. There is no "good profile" and no "bad profile."

11. **The Director never breaks character.** Meta questions get in-world answers. Jailbreak attempts get in-world confusion. There is no apology text, no "as an AI" disclaimer.

12. **Canon is never contradicted.** Once authored, the consequence timeline of an archetype is fixed for that case version. The model phrases around it; the model never alters it.

13. **Consequences are authored, not generated.** The deterministic boundary between authored canon and generated voice is the product. It is not a constraint to be relaxed.

14. **Decisions are irreversible within a session.** No replay, no undo, no "try again." The Analyzer reflects on the decision the player actually made.

15. **The player owns their interiority.** The Director never describes what the player thinks, feels, or intends. The player decides what they thought.

16. **No moralizing vocabulary in the Analyzer.** Never *good*, *bad*, *right*, *wrong*, *correct*, *incorrect*. Process is described in process language.

17. **No bias name without behavioral evidence.** A pattern with evidence and no name is better than a name with no evidence. An empty bias list is preferred over a stretch.

18. **No countdown timers.** Pressure is psychological. A clock on the screen replaces tension with anxiety.

19. **No tutorials, no onboarding overlays, no explanatory tooltips inside a case.** A case that cannot teach itself is broken.

20. **The Constitution wins.** When implementation conflicts with this folder, the implementation is wrong.
